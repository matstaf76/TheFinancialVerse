(Mostly!) AI Generated! Credit and Finance Glossary

APR (Annual Percentage Rate)
The yearly interest rate charged on borrowed money, like when you use a credit card or take out a loan.
Asset
Anything valuable that you own, like cash, a car, a house, or intellectual property.
Balance
The amount of money you have in an account, positive or negative. Includes bank accounts and amounts owed on loans or credit cards.
Bankruptcy
A legal process allowing someone who can’t pay their debts to get a fresh start, but it seriously affects credit.
Checks
A paper document issued by a bank account holder instructing the bank to pay a specific amount to the payee. Includes date, payee, amount, and signature.
Checking Account
A bank account used for daily transactions, often linked to a debit card and electronic direct deposit.
Credit Score
A number that shows how trustworthy you are at paying back borrowed money. Higher scores mean better creditworthiness.
Collateral
Something valuable promised to a lender if you can’t repay a loan, like a car or house.
Debt
Money you owe to someone else, such as a lender.
Default
Failing to repay a loan as agreed.
Equity
The value of an asset you own minus any debt owed on it. Example: a $100,000 house with $50,000 owed has 50% equity.
Foreclosure
When a lender takes back property due to default.
Fixed Rate
An interest rate that stays the same over the loan term.
Interest
Extra money you pay when borrowing or earn when saving money.
Liability
Your legal responsibility to repay debt or cover financial obligations.
Loan
Money borrowed that must be repaid, usually with interest.
Mortgage
A loan specifically for buying property, where the property is collateral.
Principal
The original amount borrowed or invested, excluding interest or fees.
Profit
The amount left after subtracting all costs from revenue.
Refinance
Replacing an existing loan with a new one, usually for a better rate.
Repossession
When a lender seizes collateral on a loan.
Risk
The chance of losing value on an investment or asset.
Savings Account
A bank account to store money and earn interest, intended for longer-term saving.
Stock
A share in ownership of a company, which can increase or decrease in value.
Term
The length of time an account or loan lasts.
Transaction
Any exchange of value, like buying or selling goods or services.
Unsecured Loan
A loan without collateral, often with higher interest due to greater risk for the lender.
Variable Rate
An interest rate that can change over time based on market conditions.
Withdrawal
Removing value from an account.
